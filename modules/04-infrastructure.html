◆ PHASE 1 RECOMMENDATION
Nili Fossae Phase 1 costs $225M less primarily because the shallow target reduces seismic survey requirements and the existing CRISM data already constrains the deposit location. Recommended: begin Phase 1 at Nili Fossae — if the H₂ + CH₄ sniffer confirms active outgassing, the case for drilling becomes extremely strong without needing the full Tharsis survey commitment.

◆ PHASE 2 KEY INSIGHT
The $1,080M drilling cost difference between sites is almost entirely driven by depth. Tharsis's 8 km shallow wildcat requires a rig nearly twice the mass of Nili Fossae's 5 km rig — and mass-to-Mars is the dominant cost driver. Nili Fossae's shallow serpentinite target is the most cost-efficient first drill on Mars.

MISSION RECOMMENDATION
Nili Fossae is the recommended Phase 1 target — $2.08B cheaper than Tharsis, produces first propellant 2 years earlier, and carries significantly lower technical risk due to its shallow drilling depth and CRISM-confirmed serpentinite evidence base.

Tharsis as Phase 2 target — the larger dome trap geometry makes it the higher-yield long-term prospect. Once Nili Fossae validates the abiogenic methane hypothesis and proves ISRU viability, Tharsis becomes the logical scale-up target with the added benefit of lessons learned from the first mission.

Combined programme total: ~$10.2B over 15 years — comparable to a single flagship NASA planetary science mission, with the potential to permanently resolve Mars ISRU propellant supply for all future crewed missions.
